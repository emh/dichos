¿Me trae los huevos estrellados, por favor?
literal Could you bring me the “starred” eggs, please?
Estrellados is the everyday word in Mexico for sunny-side-up eggs — yolk intact, whites set, never flipped. Asking “¿me trae…?” (rather than “quiero…”) is how locals stay polite without the stiffness of “quisiera.” If you want them runny, add “tiernos”; firm, “bien cocidos.”
also heard
Huevos estrellados, por favor. MX · casual Shorter, fine at a fonda or taquería counter.
¿Me pone un par de huevos a la plancha, vuelta y vuelta no? ES · Spain In Spain estrellados means broken eggs over fries. Ask for “a la plancha” and clarify that you don't want them flipped.
Quiero los huevos sin voltear. neutral Safe fallback anywhere — “without flipping.” Less idiomatic but unambiguous.

I want to order my eggs sunny side up
¿Me trae los huevos estrellados, por favor?
literal Could you bring me the “starred” eggs, please?
Mexico · neutral register · café / fonda / diner · breakfast vocabulary
When it works · and when it doesn't

Use it when

  • Sitting down at a café, fonda, or diner in Mexico for breakfast.
  • You want polite-but-natural — neither curt nor stiff.
  • You're talking to a server you don't know (use usted).

Avoid when

  • You're in Spain — there huevos estrellados means broken eggs over fries (delicious, different dish).
  • You want the yolk runny without saying so — clarify “tiernos.”
  • Talking to a friend cooking for you — drop the “¿me trae…?”, just say what you want.
In the wild · sample dialog
server
¿Qué le ofrezco para desayunar?
What can I get you for breakfast?
you
¿Me trae los huevos estrellados, por favor? Con frijoles y café americano.
Could you bring me the eggs sunny side up, please? With beans and a black coffee.
server
¿Los huevos cómo los quiere, tiernos o bien cocidos?
How do you want the eggs — runny or well-cooked?
you
Tiernos, por favor.
Runny, please.
Around the Spanish-speaking world
MX
Huevos estrellados the standard. Sunny side up, yolk intact.
ES
Huevos a la plancha (sin voltear) In Spain, estrellados is a different dish (eggs broken over fries). Specify “sin voltear.”
AR · UY
Huevos fritos, jema blanda In the Río de la Plata, plain huevos fritos with “jema blanda” (soft yolk) is clearest.
CL
Huevos a la copa (de un lado) Or simply ask for them “de un lado, sin dar vuelta.”
CO
Huevos fritos con la yema blanda Most servers will read this as sunny side up; “estrellados” works too.
